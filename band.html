Meet the band

Davy Jones

From 1966 to 1971, Jones was a member of the Monkees, a pop-rock group formed expressly for a television show of the same name. Jones also continued his acting career after the Monkees, either as himself or another character. In February 2012, Jones went to tend to his 14 horses. He complained of chest pains and was rushed to hospital, where he was pronounced dead of a severe heart attack

Michael Nesmith

Born Robert Michael Nesmith in Houston, Texas in 1942. After a tour of duty in the Air Force, Nesmith was gifted a guitar for Christmas from his parents. He joined The Monkees and was an instant success. He went on to have a very successful solo career.

Micky Dolenz

Dolenz was born in Los Angeles, California. He originally had his own rock group called "micky and the One-Nighters. He joined the television show sitcom The Monkees in 1965, and became the drummer and lead vocalist in the band. He performed with former band members in a stage production of the Harry Nilsson musical The Point! He went on to have a successful solo career.

More About

Peter Tork

Tork was born in Washington, D.C. He began studying piano at the age of nine, showing an aptitude for music by learning to play several different instruments, including banjo, accoustic bass, and guitar. He was suggested the television show Monkees by Stephen Still, who had been turned down during auditions. He enjoyed a solo career and colaborated works with his fellow band members in later days. In 2009, Tork was diagnosed with adenoid cystic carcinoma. He died from his illness in February 2019.
